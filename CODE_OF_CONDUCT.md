# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Semantix is committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and
harassment-free community for everyone, regardless of background, identity,
experience level, education, nationality, appearance, disability, religion,
sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other personal characteristic.

Participation means helping create an environment where people can learn, ask
questions, review work, disagree constructively, and contribute without
intimidation or exclusion.

## Expected behavior

Community members are expected to:

- communicate with empathy, patience, and respect;
- critique the work rather than attacking the person;
- explain disagreements with evidence and clear reasoning;
- welcome contributors with different experience levels;
- respect privacy and confidential reports;
- acknowledge mistakes and improve behavior;
- follow project security, contribution, and licensing requirements;
- prioritize community health over winning an argument.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following is not acceptable:

- harassment, threats, intimidation, or deliberate humiliation;
- discrimination, slurs, stereotypes, or exclusionary comments;
- unwanted sexual attention or sexualized language;
- personal insults, repeated hostility, or targeted disruption;
- publishing or threatening to publish private information;
- impersonation or deliberate misrepresentation;
- spam, trolling, or sustained off-topic disruption;
- pressuring contributors to reveal private data, credentials, prompts, or
  security findings;
- retaliation against good-faith reporters;
- conduct a reasonable person would consider unsafe or professionally
  inappropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, and repository content;
- project-related chat, email, calls, and documents;
- spaces where someone publicly represents Semantix;
- private communication that materially affects project participation.

## Reporting concerns

For immediate platform abuse, use GitHub's block and report tools.

For project-specific concerns, contact the maintainers privately through a
contact method listed on the
[@Dendroculus GitHub profile](https://github.com/Dendroculus). Do not open a
public issue containing sensitive personal information.

Security vulnerabilities must be reported through
[SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

Reports will be handled as confidentially as reasonably possible.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, reject, or remove contributions and communication
that violate this Code of Conduct.

Depending on severity and history, enforcement may include:

1. a correction or request to revise behavior;
2. a formal warning;
3. temporary restriction from project spaces;
4. permanent restriction from project participation.

Threats, doxxing, stalking, or credible risks of harm may be escalated to the
relevant platform or authorities.

Retaliation against a reporter or participant in an investigation is itself a
violation.
